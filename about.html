About

Hi, I'm Nav.

Data Engineer and AI Builder. Based in Newcastle with my partner and young son. I build tools that make my colleagues' work easier, and lately most of those tools have AI inside them.

What I do

I build production AI systems and the data infrastructure underneath them. That means anything from a Streamlit app with an agent loop through to the SQL Server automation that feeds it. I like the whole stack — the database migration, the embeddings, the UI, the email that goes to the CFO on Monday morning.

At Harwin I'm the person people come to when they've got data and no idea what to do with it, or an AI idea and no idea how to ship it. Both are fine starting points.

How I think about AI

Most AI projects fail in the gap between a demo and something a business will actually use every day.

I spend more time than most people on that gap. The unglamorous parts: who's the user, what happens when the model is wrong, how do you know if it's still working a month later, what do you do when someone from Finance asks a question it can't handle.

The demo is the easy part. The adoption is the hard part. I think about adoption first.

The path here

I started in telecoms doing stock forecasting at GK Telecom in Edinburgh. Did a master's at Heriot-Watt, worked on pricing engines and segmentation at a hospitality tech company, then moved into analytics leadership at ICS-Digital in Leeds — that's where I started managing a team and publishing findings in national press.

After a short spell at Brittany Ferries in the pandemic aftermath, I landed at Harwin in Newcastle. It's a traditional precision manufacturer serving aerospace, defence, and medical customers — which is exactly the kind of place where AI doesn't just magically appear. You have to build it, explain it, and earn trust for it every week.

That turned out to be the best AI Engineering classroom I could have asked for.
