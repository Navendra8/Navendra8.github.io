Harwin AI Insider · 2024

Getting 200 manufacturing staff to actually use AI in their daily work.

An internal LLM enablement programme built on the bet that sustained, role-specific content beats one-off training sessions every time.

Role Sole builder & content lead
Stack Python · HTML/CSS · GitHub Pages
Duration 24 weeks, ongoing

The problem

A traditional manufacturer serving aerospace, defence, medical, and industrial customers was sitting on mountains of operational data. Staff kept hearing about ChatGPT and Claude but didn't know how to apply them to their actual work.

Generic AI training sessions have a short half-life. People forget within a fortnight if they don't practise. The real blocker wasn't capability — it was adoption.

The approach

A sustained weekly content programme covering practical AI use cases by role:

  • Sales — using LLMs for prospect research, email drafting, call preparation.
  • Operations — supplier analysis, documentation summarisation, anomaly spotting.
  • Finance — automating monthly commentary, variance explanation, first-pass forecasting.
  • Engineering — LLM-assisted spec review, meeting note synthesis, technical writing support.

Each issue followed the same structure: one focused tip, one concrete example built on real internal scenarios, one safe-to-try prompt staff could copy and adapt.

Why it worked

Three design choices mattered. First, role-specific content — marketing doesn't care about SQL, finance doesn't care about customer research, and generic content serves neither. Second, a drip cadence — one tip a week is manageable; a monthly digest goes unread. Third, safe-to-try prompts — people learn AI by using AI, not by reading about it.

It also worked as a top-of-funnel for internal AI tooling. When DataPilot launched, staff who'd been reading the Insider for months were already primed to use it. The conceptual distance between "I read about AI helping finance teams" and "I should try this SQL assistant my colleague built" was already small.

What I'd change

The content pipeline could be more automated. Right now each issue is hand-curated. A v2 would draft candidate issues from a library of recent internal AI success stories, with me editing rather than writing from scratch.
